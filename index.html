About


Juan Manuel is passionate about technology and education, he loves using new knowledge to create useful tools that make many tasks easier.

He has participated as a member of teams with many technological projects in his work, related to the acquisition of technology to use it in his employer's activities and developing basic tools that improve the capabilities of human resources, he has also worked creating work. flow to get more efficient data for analysis.

Juan Manuel has worked under pressure due to the nature of the organization in which he worked, but this experience gave him the experience and discipline that make him a person truly committed to fulfilling the mission.


Philosophy


Juan Manuel loves working for and with the community. He thinks that it is very important to help people, but the best way to help is not just to give the fish, it is more useful to teach how to fish.

That's why he loves teaching and everything that surrounds education... (smiling) And his wife is also a primary school teacher in a rural area of ​​Colombia.


Contributions


Juan Manuel has served as a Noncommissioned Officer in the Colombian Army for 13 years, his last rank was Sergeant, his work has been focused on developing analysis on tracking the location of threats for Colombian territory with photogrammetry and Terrain Analysis

Later he left the army and worked as a software developer in Digital Marketing companies but continued to work for the community through programs such as "Catalina Muñoz" dedicated to building houses for families in poor conditions.

Juan Manuel was invited to return to the army, but this time he uses his new knowledge about technology to develop a tool that helps the military engineers of the Humanitarian Demining Brigade to order the best way to clear land of mines and explosives, that were planted in rural areas by armed groups during the armed conflict.

For the last four years, he has worked with distributed computing, big data, and data science as a scrum master for these projects, helping the scrum team to have all the tools that allow them to develop software that makes the work of the Colombian Army easier and faster. During this time he got the opportunity to qualify and be awarded to the DS4A program to train the first 300 data scientists in Colombia by an organization called Correlation One and the MinTIC.


Wishes

Juan Manuel is committed to creating tools that make human life easier and more relevant. That is why he would like to improve his knowledge about Big Data, Data Science and Machine Learning, put these tools into practice for the community through Software, perhaps IoT hardware, and improve the education of our children by giving them an education with human values, idioms and technology.
